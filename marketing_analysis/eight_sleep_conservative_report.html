Channel Performance Overview

üîç Google Ads - The Harvester

Google Ads appears to excel at capturing high-intent customers who are ready to purchase. With 71% of total marketing spend ($10.3M) and consistent monthly order generation, this channel demonstrates characteristics of harvesting demand created by other marketing efforts rather than creating new demand. The consistent performance across months suggests Google captures customers actively searching for sleep solutions, likely influenced by awareness campaigns from YouTube and other channels.
Total Investment: $10.3M (71% of spend)
Performance Pattern: Consistent monthly orders
Strategic Role: High-intent demand harvester
Critical Analysis Need: Search vs Shopping vs Brand breakdown

üì∫ YouTube Ads - The Reach & Email Engine

YouTube Ads demonstrates exceptional capability in two key areas: massive reach generation and high-quality email capture. With monthly spend varying dramatically from $1K to $811K, this channel shows the most volatile investment pattern but delivers unique value through email acquisition. Email captures from YouTube convert at rates up to 1.67% within 60 days - significantly higher than other channels. This suggests YouTube attracts prospects early in their customer journey who require nurturing before purchase, making it an ideal awareness and lead generation channel rather than a direct conversion driver.
Total Investment: $2.3M (16% of spend)
Spend Pattern: Highly variable ($1K-$811K)
Email Capture Peak: 5.26% capture rate
Email Conversion: Up to 1.67% (60-day)
Strategic Role: Awareness + Lead generation
Critical Test Need: Email optimization vs direct conversion

üì± Facebook Ads - The Steady Performer

Facebook Ads stands out for its remarkable consistency and reliability across the 18-month period. With $2.2M in total spend (15% allocation), Facebook demonstrates the most predictable performance patterns of all paid channels. While email capture rates are similar to YouTube (averaging 3.24%), the email-to-conversion rates are roughly half, suggesting Facebook captures different types of prospects. The steady, consistent performance across months indicates Facebook could be the most reliable channel for scaling new customer acquisition, but this requires incrementality testing to validate true additional impact beyond what would occur organically.
Total Investment: $2.2M (15% of spend)
Performance Pattern: Most consistent across months
Email Capture: 3.24% average rate
Email Conversion: 0.42% (60-day)
Strategic Role: Reliable scaling candidate
Critical Test Need: Lift study for incrementality proof
